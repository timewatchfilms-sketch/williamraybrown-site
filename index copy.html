The Interference

In 1974, someone filed a patent. US3951134. Apparatus and method for remotely monitoring and altering brain waves. The technology was real. Then it went quiet.

James Harlan knows what the inside of that silence feels like. He spent eight years wired into an experimental neural mesh, thoughts shared across light-years with a team of four minds working in concert. Problems turned beautiful when they held them together. Then came the months of unlearning how to tell his thoughts from theirs. At twenty-nine he signed the removal papers. He has not synced since.

That was a decade ago. Then a message arrives. Colonists at Arcadia Base on Mars are hearing voices. Some report euphoric music lifting them toward something they cannot name. Others describe commands. The Colonial Authority needs someone who knows the hardware from the inside. Someone who understands the difference between a system behaving as designed and one that has been turned against the people it was built to serve.

What Harlan finds sixty million miles from home is not a malfunction. The neural mesh connecting every settler at Arcadia Base is working exactly as intended. The question is who is holding the key.

The questions that drive every book started a long time before the first page. Mathematics as a child. Physics as a framework. Consciousness as the thing neither fully explains. Thousands of hours spent in the gap between what the equations say and what they refuse to say.

We were not born too late to explore the universe. We were born at the exact moment to uncover its hidden keys.

The books that come out of that inquiry are not invented. They are researched. Real patent filings. Declassified CIA documents. Quantum entanglement papers. Retrocausality studies that did not make it into the textbooks. The conspiracy in these pages is never the writer's idea. The writer just follows the documents to where they actually lead.

The Malech patent is real. US3951134. Filed in 1974 by the United States government. Apparatus and method for remotely monitoring and altering brain waves. It was approved. It is sitting in the public record. That is not the premise of a novel. That is the starting point of one.

The thread connecting all of it, Mars and Europa and simulation theory and parallel realities, is a single question that has never been satisfactorily answered: what exactly is consciousness, who has access to it, and what happens when someone else holds the key. My books are the attempt to find out. The rabbit hole goes quite far down. I'll extend the ladder.
